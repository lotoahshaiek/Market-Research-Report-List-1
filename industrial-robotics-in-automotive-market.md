<p><p>Industrial robotics in the automotive market encompasses various robot types, each serving distinct functions. Articulated robots, featuring flexible joints, excel in complex assembly tasks. Cartesian robots utilize linear motions along three axes, ideal for precision tasks like material handling. SCARA robots, with their two parallel arms, are efficient for assembly and packaging. Cylindrical robots offer movements within cylindrical coordinates, suitable for welding. Parallel robots, characterized by a closed-loop structure, deliver high speed and precision in assembly. Other types may include collaborative robots, enhancing human-machine interaction.</p></p>
